Weekly Assignments Final Project About Me
Week 5: 3D Design + Printing

Task: 3D Print something that could not be created through substractive methods


Skills used: Rhino 3D Modelling, Prusa 3D Printer, Metashape scanning software


This week I was inspired by MC Escher’s spiral drawings. This is something that could not be easily made via the subtractive method because of the way the object wraps around itself. While I did not copy MC Escher’s design exactly, I chose to make a spiral that gets smaller and wraps around itself.




Process: The spiral was modeled in Rhino. The print time was 17 hours, in part because my design required a lot of support material.

I attempted to make an additional spiral that failed twice in the printer. I believe this is because it is wound tightly, leaving small spaces where I believe the printer attempted to print support material and could not.

Printing Test: As a hobby when I am not in graduate school, I enjoy wood carving of intricate details. I was curious to see to what level of detail the Prusa could print a typical pattern that would be carved. Using 3D models that I found on the internet, I printed 2 tests. They turned out fairly smooth, although the lines formed from the printing process are highly visible.

Scan: I scanned my 3D printed spiral. As a next step, it would have been interesting to reprint form the scan to see how accurate it was.
